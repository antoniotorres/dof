# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
this project a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This project adopts the **[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/),
version 2.1**. The summary below is a quick reference; the linked document is
the authoritative text.

## Our standards

Behavior that helps create a positive environment:

- Showing empathy and kindness toward others.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Taking responsibility, apologizing for mistakes, and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.

Behavior that is not tolerated:

- Insulting, demeaning, or derogatory comments, and personal or political
  attacks.
- Public or private harassment of any kind.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Unwelcome attention or advances.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards and will take fair, appropriate corrective action in response to any
behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (issues, pull requests,
discussions, and code) and when an individual is officially representing the
project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported privately to the project
maintainer at **toniotgz@gmail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and
investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the
privacy and security of anyone who reports an incident.
